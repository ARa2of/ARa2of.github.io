About



    Welcome 

    I am a research PhD candidate at Energy, Power and Intelligent Control (EPIC) research cluster, Queen's University Belfast. Motivated by the energy evolution towards low carbon communities in accordance with the net zero carbon emissions targets and the sustainable development goals. Hence, my research focuses on the integration of renewable energy and low carbon technologies by investigating the invovative feasible solutions that can be applied in order to enhance the operation, performance, and stability of the electrical power networks.

    • My PhD thesis aims to facilitate the integration of renewable energy using energy storage systems and improving the distribution network performance to accommodate securely more low carbon technologies as a part of SPIRE2 Project.
    • Recipient of the IEEE Region 8 Student Paper Contest First Award 2018
    • Formerly, an assistant lecturer at the Electrical & Control Department of Arab Academy for Science, Technology & Maritime Transport, Cairo Campus (AAST) with more than 3 years of experience in the field of teaching, research and development on electrical power and energy technology.
    • Major Research Areas: Power Systems, Distribution Networks, Renewable Energy Resources, Distributed Generation, Energy Storage Systems, Electrical Vehicle, Active Distribution Networks, Demand Side Management and Optimization.


    Latest Projects : Developed Tools, Algorithms and Strategies

    • Residential Battery Management Tool (RBMT)
      • A tool of three power management strategies for the domestic battery in order to maximize the customers profitability and support the network operation. (Link)
    • Maximizing Market Revenue Tool (MMRTool)
      • A tool to maximize the revenues from the participation of batteries in the energy marked of the integrated single electricity market of Ireland (SEMO).
    • Grid power levelling Strategy
      • A strategy combined of real-time and look-ahead scheduling of battery energy storage systems to improve the network operation by flattening the grid power profile.
    • Battery Energy Storage Systems Allocation and Sizing
      • Two algorithms are developed to determine the optimal battery capacity to solve the network violations and enhance the performance of the medium and low voltage networks.
    • Battery Energy Storage Systems Active Reactive Power Control
      • A model predictive control is developed to control the active and reactive power dispatch of the battery inverter to support the network operation.
