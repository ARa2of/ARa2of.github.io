About





Welcome 

I am a research PhD candidate at Energy, Power and Intelligent Control (EPIC) research cluster, Queen's University Belfast. Motivated by the energy evolution towards low carbon future following the net-zero carbon targets and the sustainable development goals. Researching on the integration of renewable energy, energy storage systems, and low carbon technologies by investigating the innovative feasible solutions that can be applied to enhance the operation, performance, and stability of the electrical power networks.

  • My PhD thesis aims to facilitate the integration of renewable energy using energy storage systems and improving the distribution network performance to accommodate securely more low carbon technologies as a part of EU SPIRE2 Project.
  • Recipient of the 1st Prize Award in the Graduate Student Poster Contest at the 2021 IEEE PES GM.
  • Recipient of the 1st Prize Award in IEEE Region 8 Student Paper Contest 2018.
  • Formerly, an assistant lecturer at the Electrical & Control Department of Arab Academy for Science, Technology & Maritime Transport, Cairo Campus (AAST), Egypt with more than 6 years of experience in the field of teaching, research and development on electrical power and energy technology.
  • Major Research Areas: Power Systems, Distribution Networks, Renewable Energy Resources, Distributed Generation, Energy Storage Systems, Electrical Vehicle, Active Distribution Networks, Demand Side Management and Optimization.

Projects 

  • Residential battery techno-economic analysis
    • Comprehensive analysis on the behind the meter batteries in terms of scheduling and operation, degradation, and profitability analysis in the presence of EV and PV.  The project includes analysing the measurements of a real battery installed in SPIRE 2 project testbed, Northern Ireland. 
  • I-SEM forecasting and energy arbitrage maximization framework
    • A forecasting model for the integrated single electricity market (I-SEM) of the island of Ireland and an energy arbitrage maximization framework to maximize the revenues from the participation of batteries in I-SEM ex-ante markets
  • Grid power levelling Framework
    • A frameowrk combined of two strategies; real-time and look-ahead scheduling of battery energy storage systems to improve the distribution network operation by flattening the grid power curve.
  • Battery Energy Storage Systems Allocation and Sizing
    • Two algorithms are developed to determine the optimal battery capacity to solve the network violations and enhance the performance of the medium and low voltage networks.
  • Battery Energy Storage Systems Active Reactive Power Control
    • A model predictive control is developed to control the active and reactive power dispatch of the battery inverter to support the network operation by enhancing the power quality while maintaining the network constraints within the acceptable limits.
  • Power Management Strategy for the Active Distribution Networks
    • A two stage strategy is developed using OPF and Multi-agent systems in order to optimize the operation of smart grids with high penetrations of renewable energy.
